The Monetization Engine

Understanding what drives in-game purchases is crucial. Our analysis reveals that spending is not random; it's deeply tied to player engagement. The more time and effort players invest, the higher their likelihood to spend.

Key Predictors of Spending:

  • 🕒 Increased PlayTimeHours
  • 📈 Higher EngagementLevel

RPG Genre Leads In-Game Purchases

The Complex Relationship Between Genre, Difficulty & Playtime

Does "Hard" difficulty always mean more playtime? Our Two-Way ANOVA results show it's not that simple. The effect of difficulty is strongly influenced by the game's genre, creating a unique interaction that designers must consider to maximize engagement.

The non-parallel lines vividly illustrate the significant interaction effect: the impact of difficulty changes drastically from one genre to another.

Defining Player Engagement Levels

Using multinomial logistic regression, we've categorized players into three distinct engagement levels—Low, Medium, and High—based on their playtime and session frequency. This segmentation is crucial for targeted communication and feature development.

Low Engagement (30%)

Players with lower playtime and fewer weekly sessions. Potential for growth with re-engagement campaigns.

Medium Engagement (50%)

The core of our player base. Consistent and regular, they are key to community stability.

High Engagement (20%)

Our 'power users'. They play the most and most frequently, driving community trends and providing valuable feedback.

Uncovering Player Archetypes

K-Means clustering analysis on playtime and session data has revealed three distinct player archetypes. This scatter plot visualizes these segments, providing a clear map of our player landscape.

Archetype 1: The Casual Dabbler

Characterized by low weekly sessions and shorter playtimes. They enjoy quick, accessible gameplay and are sensitive to high difficulty.

Archetype 2: The Dedicated Regular

Forms the backbone of our community. They have high session frequency but moderate playtime per session. They value routine and consistent updates.

Archetype 3: The Hardcore Loyalist

Our most invested players, with high total playtime and frequent sessions. They are often purchasers and thrive on challenge and complex game mechanics.

Actionable Recommendations

Incentivize First-Time Purchasers

Since purchasers play significantly more, create targeted, high-value "starter packs" for engaged non-purchasers (e.g., high playtime, Archetype 2) to convert them and deepen their investment.

Tailor Content by Archetype

Develop different content streams: quick, low-commitment events for 'Casual Dabblers'; consistent, rewarding daily quests for 'Dedicated Regulars'; and complex, high-challenge raids or modes for 'Hardcore Loyalists'.

Optimize Genre & Difficulty

Recognize that 'Hard' difficulty in RPGs attracts our most dedicated players. Invest in robust, challenging endgame content for these genres, while ensuring accessible entry points in genres like Puzzle and Simulation to attract new players.
